Report a change of circumstances if you have a visa or BRP
<h1>You're in the UK and have a BRP</h1>
<p>You must report any changes in your circumstances if you’re in the UK and have either:</p>
<li>got a biometric residence permit (BRP)</li>
<li>applied for a BRP but have not had a decision yet</li>
<p>How you do this depends on what you’re reporting.</p>
<h2>Report a change of address only</h2>
<p>You can change your address without having to apply for a new BRP.</p>
<p>You can either:</p>
<li>report a change of address online</li>
<li>fill in the change of circumstances form and send it to the address on the form</li>
<h2>Report a change to your name or personal details</h2>
<p>You must apply for a new BRP straight away if any of these things change:</p>
<li>name, for example if you’ve got married</li>
<li>nationality</li>
<li>facial appearance</li>
<li>date of birth, for example if it was wrong</li>
<li>gender</li>
<p>Apply for a replacement BRP online. You’ll need to pay a fee.</p>
<p>You must apply for a new BRP within 3 months. You can be fined up to £1,000 or have your stay shortened if you do not.</p>
<h2>Report all other changes</h2>
<p>You must report any other changes to the details you gave in your BRP application, including if:</p>
<li>you get a criminal conviction</li>
<li>you separate from your partner</li>
<li>any of your children stop living permanently with you</li>
<p>Fill in the change of circumstances form and send it to the address on the form.</p>
<h1>You're in the UK and have a visa</h1>
<p>You must report any changes if you’re in the UK and have either:</p>
<li>got a visa</li>
<li>applied for a visa but haven’t had a decision yet</li>
<p>How you do this depends on what you’re reporting.</p>
<h2>Changes to your name or personal details</h2>
<p>You must transfer your visa to a biometric residence permit (BRP) if your overall stay in the UK is longer than 6 months and any of these details on your visa document change:</p>
<li>name</li>
<li>nationality</li>
<li>facial appearance</li>
<li>date of birth</li>
<li>gender</li>
<p>You’ll need to pay a fee.</p>
<p>You do not need to report these changes or transfer your visa if your stay in the UK is less than 6 months.</p>
<p>Fill in a change of circumstances form if you’ve applied for a visa but haven’t had a decision.</p>
<h2>Report all other changes</h2>
<p>Fill in the change of circumstances form to report any other changes of circumstances including:</p>
<li>your contact details</li>
<li>your legal representative’s details</li>
<li>dependent family members’ details</li>
<li>if you separate from your partner</li>
<li>if you get a criminal conviction</li>
<li>if any of your children stop living with you</li>
<h3>Report a change of address only</h3>
<p>You can update your address or your legal representative’s address online.</p>
<h1>You're outside the UK</h1>
<p>How you report a change of circumstances depends on how you applied and the status of your application.</p>
<h2>If you have a UK Visas and Immigration account</h2>
<p>You will have a UK Visas and Immigration account if you used the ‘UK Immigration: ID Check’ smartphone app to scan your identity document on your phone.</p>
<h3>You’re still waiting for a decision about your application</h3>
<p>You can change your email address or phone number by updating your account details. You cannot update your identity document, name or address if you’re still waiting for a decision.</p>
<h3>You have a decision about your application</h3>
<p>You can update your account details.</p>
<h2>If you applied through a visa application centre</h2>
<p>Contact the visa application centre where you applied if you’re outside the UK and there’s a change to your:</p>
<li>reason for going to the UK</li>
<li>address</li>
<li>personal details, for example your name, because you got married</li>
<p>You may need to make another visa application at your local visa application  centre.</p>
